Understanding Player Ratings

Player ratings in NBA 2K20 APK are like grades in school, but for different basketball skills. They cover areas like shooting, dribbling, defense, and more. Each player has a rating for each skill, typically on a scale from 0 to 99. A higher rating means the player is better at that particular skill. Let’s break down some of the key ratings:

Shooting

  • Three-Point Shooting (3PT): Indicates how well a player can shoot from beyond the arc. A high rating here means more successful three-pointers.
  • Mid-Range Shooting: Reflects a player's accuracy in shooting from mid-range.
  • Free Throw (FT): Shows how reliable a player is at making free throws.

Dribbling

  • Ball Handle: Determines a player’s ability to dribble and control the ball. Higher ratings reduce the chances of losing the ball.
  • Speed with Ball: Combines speed and dribbling skills to measure how fast a player can move while dribbling.

Defense

  • Perimeter Defense: Measures a player's ability to defend against opponents on the outside.
  • Interior Defense: Reflects how well a player can defend close to the basket.
  • Steal: Indicates how effectively a player can steal the ball from opponents.

Physical Attributes

  • Speed: Overall speed of the player on the court.
  • Strength: Measures a player’s physical power, useful for rebounding and defending.
  • Vertical: Indicates how high a player can jump, important for blocking and dunking.

Exploring Player Stats

Player stats provide a numerical summary of a player’s performance in actual games. Here are some key stats you should know:

Scoring

  • Points Per Game (PPG): The average number of points a player scores in a game.
  • Field Goal Percentage (FG%): The percentage of successful shots made out of all attempts.
  • Three-Point Percentage (3P%): The success rate of three-point shots.

Rebounding

  • Rebounds Per Game (RPG): The average number of rebounds a player grabs per game.
  • Offensive Rebounds: Rebounds collected on the offensive end, giving the team another chance to score.
  • Defensive Rebounds: Rebounds collected on the defensive end, preventing the opponent from scoring.

Assists and Playmaking

  • Assists Per Game (APG): The average number of assists a player makes per game, indicating playmaking ability.
  • Turnovers: The number of times a player loses the ball to the opponent.

Defense

  • Steals Per Game (SPG): The average number of times a player successfully steals the ball per game.
  • Blocks Per Game (BPG): The average number of shots a player blocks per game.
